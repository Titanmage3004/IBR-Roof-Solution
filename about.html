More about our products

Our components are manufactured from high‑grade, UV‑stabilized polypropylene — a material selected for its excellent impact resistance, low moisture absorption, and long‑term dimensional stability in outdoor environments. This makes our parts durable, lightweight, and resistant to corrosion.

Products are injection‑molded to tight tolerances so they mate cleanly with standard IBR sheeting and fasteners. Polypropylene resists rust and chemical degradation, reducing maintenance and extending the service life of repairs.

Key material features:

  • UV‑stabilized polypropylene for long outdoor life
  • Corrosion‑resistant — will not rust
  • Lightweight and impact‑resistant for easy handling
  • Thermally stable across typical roof temperature ranges
  • Recyclable and low‑maintenance
Inner Rib Block

With the Inner Rib Block:

Installing IBR sheeting using only screws may seem sufficient at first, but over time this method can lead to significant roof damage.

As the roof expands and contracts due to changing temperatures, the area around the screw becomes prone to wear, loosening the fixings and creating vulnerability to water penetration.

Additionally, the pressure of the screw can cause dents in the sheeting, disrupting the natural flow of rainwater and increasing the risk of pooling and leaks.

To mitigate these issues, the use of an Inner Rib Block is essential. When installed alongside the IBR sheeting and screw, the Inner Rib Block distributes pressure evenly, maintaining the integrity of the sheet and reducing stress on the fixings.

This method not only enhances the durability of the roof but also significantly reduces the need for ongoing maintenance.

Without the Inner Rib Block:

When IBR sheeting is fixed using only screws, the load concentrates around the fastener point which over time leads to material fatigue and potential leaks.

Without a supporting block, the sheeting can deform and the screw may loosen as thermal movement occurs, increasing the risk of water ingress and structural damage.

This approach requires more frequent maintenance and can shorten the lifespan of the roof sheeting compared with installations that use the Inner Rib Block.

Upper Roof Support Bracket

Cost-Effective Roof Repair Without Full Sheet Replacement

Instead of replacing your entire roof sheeting, you can now minimize repair costs with a simple and effective solution. The combination of the Upper Roof Support Bracket and the Inner Rib Block provides a practical DIY alternative for addressing localized damage caused by rusted screws or sheet corrosion.

Over time, rust around screw points can deteriorate the sheeting, creating holes that lead to leaks. This solution allows you to repair only the affected areas, restoring structural integrity and preventing further water damage.

Follow these steps for a cost-effective repair:

  • Remove the old, rusted screw.
  • Cut out the damaged section of the sheeting.
  • Prepare a replacement piece of roof sheeting, ideally 40 mm larger than the area removed.
  • Install the new sheeting using the Inner Rib Block, Upper Roof Support Bracket (as shown in the image), and a new screw.
  • Paint and seal the repaired section to protect against future corrosion.

By implementing this method, you can extend the lifespan of your roof, reduce maintenance requirements, and avoid the costs of full roof replacement.
